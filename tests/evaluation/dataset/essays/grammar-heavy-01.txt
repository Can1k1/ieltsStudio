Many young peoples is struggling to find employment after they graduate from university. Some peoples argue that universities should focus more on practical skills and less on academic theory. I agree with this view to some extent.

Firstly, companies wants graduates who can do work immediately. For example, a IT company need workers who can coding without long training. If university teach more practical skills, graduate can start work fast.

Secondly, academic theory are sometimes not useful in real jobs. Students spend many years to learn theory but they dont know how to apply it. This are a waste of time and money for students and society.

However, theory are still important in some fields like medicine and law. Doctors and lawyers must study theory before they practice.

In conclusion, universities should focus more on practical skills for most students because this help employment, but they should keep theory in important subjects.
